## 📄 论文 1 - 相关性: 45%
**标题:** TDMM-LM: Bridging Facial Understanding and Animation via Language Models
**作者:** Luchuan Song, Pinxin Liu, Haiyang Liu
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16936

**相关性说明:** While both papers are in Computer Graphics and deal with motion generation, there is a significant domain mismatch. The research profile focuses on VIDEO-TO-MOTION pipelines (extracting 3D human motion from 2D videos), while this paper addresses TEXT-TO-FACIAL-MOTION generation (using language models for facial animation from text). The paper doesn't address the core pain points: extracting visual guidance features from 2D videos, bridging 2D video priors to 3D motion, or quality supervision for video-to-motion pipelines. The methods also differ - this paper uses language models for facial motion, not VLMs for video understanding or video generation models like the profile mentions. It's moderately relevant as facial motion is a subset of human motion, but doesn't match the specific video-to-motion pipeline focus.

**摘要:** 本文提出利用生成式基础模型合成大规模面部行为语料库，并通过将3D面部参数建模为语言问题，实现文本与面部动作的双向转换（Motion2Language和Language2Motion），为文本条件下面部动画和动作理解建立了统一框架。


## 📄 论文 2 - 相关性: 45%
**标题:** OnlineHMR: Video-based Online World-Grounded Human Mesh Recovery
**作者:** Yiwen Zhao, Ce Zheng, Yufu Wang
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17355

**相关性说明:** This paper addresses video-based 3D human mesh recovery (estimating 3D body from video), which is related to the research profile's interest in video-to-motion/mesh pipelines. However, there's a key distinction: the research profile focuses on VIDEO-TO-MOTION GENERATION (creating/synthesizing motion), while this paper is about MOTION RECOVERY/ESTIMATION (extracting existing motion from video). Additionally, this paper doesn't address the specific pain points mentioned: quality control mechanisms, quality supervision, or filtering. It focuses on online/real-time processing, causal reasoning, and world grounding - not on generation quality control. The methods also differ: profile mentions video generation models (万相, Seedance), VLM, while this paper uses dual-branch architecture with causal key-value cache and SLAM.

**摘要:** OnlineHMR是一个完全在线的框架,能从单目视频中实时恢复3D人体网格、人体轨迹和运动重建。该方法满足了在线处理的四个关键标准:系统级因果性、真实性、时间一致性和效率。


## 📄 论文 3 - 相关性: 45%
**标题:** AHOY! Animatable Humans under Occlusion from YouTube Videos with Gaussian Splatting and Video Diffusion Priors
**作者:** Aymen Mir, Riza Alp Guler, Xiangjun Tang
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17975

**相关性说明:** The paper is in Computer Graphics but addresses avatar RECONSTRUCTION from occluded videos, not video-to-motion GENERATION. While it uses video diffusion priors and Gaussian Splatting (related to visual features/3D), the core task is different - it's about reconstructing 3D avatars that can be animated with new poses, not generating motion sequences from video. It partially addresses "quality supervision" via diffusion hallucination, but misses the motion generation focus entirely. The application domain mismatch (reconstruction vs motion generation) makes it moderately relevant at best.

**摘要:** AHOY是一种从严重遮挡的单目视频中重建完整、可动画的3D高斯头像的方法，解决了现有方法无法处理真实世界遮挡场景的问题。

**作者:** Rui Hong, Jana Kosecka
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17396

**相关性说明:** The paper focuses on 3D hand pose ESTIMATION from single RGB images (recovering existing pose), while the research profile is about video-to-motion GENERATION (creating new 3D motion from video). While both involve hand motion, this paper addresses recovering pose from images rather than generating motion sequences from video, which is what the profile seeks. **摘要:** 本文提出利用手势语义作为归纳偏置来从单目RGB图像估计3D手势姿态的两阶段框架，包括手势感知预训练和Transformer引导的关节回归。


## 📄 论文 5 - 相关性: 35%

**相关性说明:** This paper addresses skeleton understanding and action recognition using MLLMs - it converts skeleton sequences to visual images for understanding. However, the research profile is about VIDEO-TO-MOTION generation (extracting 3D motion from 2D videos), which is a fundamentally different application domain. This paper works in the opposite direction (skeleton→understanding) rather than video→motion generation. While both involve VLMs, the application domains are different: motion understanding vs. motion generation from video.
